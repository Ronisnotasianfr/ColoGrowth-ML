# Suggested Reviewers

## 1. Dr. Anita Sveen
- **Affiliation**: Department of Molecular Oncology, Institute for Cancer Research, Oslo University Hospital
- **Why relevant**: Dr. Sveen is the principal author of the ColoGuideEx and ColoGuidePro gene expression prognostic signatures for stage II and III colorectal cancer — the benchmark approach directly compared to in Table 9 of this manuscript. Her expertise spans microarray-based molecular subtyping and prognostic gene signatures in COAD.
- **Representative publication**: Agesen, T. H., Sveen, A., et al. ColoGuideEx: a robust gene classifier specific for stage II colorectal cancer prognosis. *Clinical Cancer Research*, 2012. DOI: 10.1158/1078-0432.CCR-11-2097

---

## 2. Dr. Laetitia Marisa
- **Affiliation**: Programme Cartes d'Identité des Tumeurs, Ligue Nationale Contre le Cancer, Paris, France
- **Why relevant**: Dr. Marisa is the lead author of the landmark GEO GSE39582 microarray study (the primary training cohort used in this manuscript), which defined six molecular CRC subtypes with distinct survival characteristics. Her expertise in CRC transcriptomics is directly relevant.
- **Representative publication**: Marisa, L. et al. Gene expression classification of colon cancer into molecular subtypes with distinct clinical and survival characteristics. *PLoS Medicine*, 2013.

---

## 3. Dr. Giovanni Parmigiani
- **Affiliation**: Dana-Farber Cancer Institute / Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, Boston, MA
- **Why relevant**: A leading biostatistician and computational oncologist known for work on cancer genomics, prediction models, and multi-cohort validation. His expertise is highly relevant to evaluating the statistical rigor of our bootstrap CI and Cox PH modeling approaches.
- **Representative publication**: Trippa, L., & Parmigiani, G. Bayesian approaches to clinical trial design. *Statistical Science*, 2017.
